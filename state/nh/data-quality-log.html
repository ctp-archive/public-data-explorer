May 14, 2020 to May 15, 2020

Variables affected:

  • negative

Tags: CTP Backfill State Annotation / Source Change

On May 15, 2020 New Hampshire stopped reporting Negative PCR tests and we began calculating Negatives as Total PCR tests (people) - Total cases. New Hampshire also split out reporting on Antibody and PCR Tests at the same time. Updating May 14, 2020 to match the screenshot, and May 15, 2020 to match the calculation Total PCR tests (people) - Total cases, this update will cause negatives to decrease by about 300.

April 29, 2020 to December 25, 2020

Variables affected:

  • probableCases
  • positiveTestsPeopleAntigen
  • positiveCasesViral

Tags: CTP Backfill State Annotation / Source Change

NH provides a breakdown between antigen and PCR confirmed cases. We started capturing the antigen number on November 20, 2020. It's clear that these antigens are revised down upon PCR confirmation because, until December 13, 2020, NH provided the figures of individuals who were reclassified to PCR as a separate number. However, we currently capture the lumped numbers in Confirmed cases, the antigen numbers in positive antigen tests (people), and nothing in probable cases. This change is to start capturing PCR only as a confirmed number and antigen as a probable number and delete the antigen positive number since these are revised downward.
